About Noble Slate

Now

Hi. I am George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This site is where I keep track of what I am actually building and learning. It doubles as a logbook for future me. If I worked on something, it should be here somewhere. If I am still figuring it out, I will say that instead of pretending it is finished.

The name Noble Slate comes out of my own name. “George” traces back to a Greek root for an “earth worker” or farmer (see this etymology). “Owen” connects to names that are usually glossed as “well born” or “noble” (Owen entry). Slate is a fine-grained rock that splits into flat sheets and used to be a literal writing surface in schools (background on slate). So I read it as: put in the work, try to be steady, and keep the page re writable when new information shows up.[1]

Right now

I am drafting a thrust-vector-controlled model rocket that I can actually recover after flight. I am sketching a two-servo gimbal, mapping out a PID controller, picking an IMU, and writing the first control loop and ground test checklist. When it fails, the failure notes will go here, not in a private notebook.

I am also developing an early wearable health device called VitaTrace. I have a first-pass concept, a simple service flow, and a list of what it should measure and report. My father died from pancreatic cancer when I was younger, so I care a lot about small early signals that are easy to miss but matter later. VitaTrace is my way of taking that personally and trying to turn it into work.

On the music side, I am getting more confident on tuba. I keep a practice log for breath work, long tones, and time on the horn. There is also a beginner-friendly tuba page on this site (basic tuba overview) that I update as I learn what actually helps.[2]

Last updated: November 07, 2025.

I write pages in Markdown and publish plain HTML. The CSS is small and hand written (MDN CSS docs) so the site stays fast and readable on weak connections and older hardware. GitHub Pages serves the files and Cloudflare sits in front of it. It is simple on purpose so the work on the page matters more than the stack.[3]
