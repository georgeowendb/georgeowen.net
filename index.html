Now

My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my small corner of the internet. It’s part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I built something, broke something, or learned something, it should show up here. If I’m still learning, I’ll say that clearly.

This site is called Noble Slate for a reason. George traces back to a Greek root for an “earth worker”. Owen is usually linked to “well-born” or “noble”. A slate is both a metamorphic rock that holds a straight line under pressure and a surface you write on, erase, and write on again.

Put that together and you get roughly how I want to work: grounded, under some pressure, and willing to rewrite things in public. That’s what this page is for.

Right now

Rocket

I’m sketching a simple model rocket with thrust vector control that I can actually recover in one piece. On paper right now:

  • a two-servo gimbal idea for the motor,
  • a rough PID controller outline,
  • a short-list for an IMU,
  • a first control-loop sketch and a ground test plan.

When it fails (it will), I’ll post the failure notes here instead of pretending it was smooth.

VitaTrace

I’m also drafting an early wearable health concept called VitaTrace. The goal: track useful signals early, not just label people as “sick” or “fine”. I’m writing:

  • a clear problem statement in normal language,
  • a basic service flow for how someone would actually use it,
  • notes on privacy and health data that make sense for Ontario.

My dad died of pancreatic cancer when I was very young. That’s the reason I care about early signals instead of vague “health tech” buzzwords.

Music

I’m getting more solid on tuba. I track what I actually do: breath work, long tones, time on the instrument, and small notes that a beginner could follow without wanting to give up. Eventually I’d like to turn that into a short, honest guide for other students.
