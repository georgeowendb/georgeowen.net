About Noble Slate

Now

Hello there. My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my corner of this internet. It is part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I did something, it will be shown here. If I am still learning, I will say that instead of pretending I have finished.

This site is called Noble Slate for a simple reason: George links back to an old Greek root for “earth worker”.[6] Owen is usually tied to forms of Eugenius, read as “well-born” or “noble”.[7] A slate is a metamorphic rock that holds a straight line when you split it, and it is also the surface you write on, erase, and write on again.[8][9] Put it together and you get how I want to work: do the ground-level work, stay steady under pressure, and be willing to rewrite things in public. That is the point of this page.

Right now

Rocket

I am drafting a simple model rocket with thrust vector control that I can recover cleanly. I am sketching a two-servo gimbal, a first control loop, and a short ground test plan. I am reading about how thrust vectoring actually works in real vehicles instead of guessing.[10][11][12] I keep a short list of IMUs, and I am getting used to PID controllers and what happens when you tune them badly.[13][14][15][16] When the rocket fails (and it will, at least once), the failure notes will be linked here.

VitaTrace

I am currently developing an early wearable health concept called VitaTrace. The whole point is simple: can we spot useful signals earlier, especially for diseases like pancreatic cancer that are usually found late?[17][18][19][20][21] I am writing:

  • a clear problem statement in normal language,
  • a basic service and data flow for how it would actually be used,
  • and first-pass privacy notes for Ontario.

My father passed away from pancreatic cancer when I was very young. That is why I care about early signals and actual patient journeys, not just saying “I like medicine” on a form.

Music

I am currently getting more proficient at tuba. I log breath work, long tones, time, and what breaks down when I get tired. I rewrite notes so a beginner could follow them without wanting to quit after a week. In concert band I have played euphonium in the past and tuba now.[22]

A running list of what I am doing “right now” will live on a small /now page, similar in spirit to the nownownow idea.[23]

Old stuff

When I clean older notes, they will go here as “Deprecated”. They will stay up with a short note about what I would do differently now, instead of just being quietly deleted.

About me

I am a Grade 10 student in the Extended French program at Holy Trinity Catholic High School in Simcoe, Ontario.[1][2][3][4][5] I am trying to build a clear record of what I learn and build, instead of only saying that I am “interested” in things.

At school, I am involved in:

  • Student Council
  • HTTV announcements and A/V (soundboard and TriCaster)
  • Concert band (euphonium in the past, tuba currently)
  • Health care club
  • Knitting club
  • Newspaper club

Outside of school, I am in the Royal Canadian Air Cadets through a local squadron, which is part of the national cadet program run with the Canadian Armed Forces.[6][7][8] I like that it forces you to show up, listen, and make decisions in real time, not just talk about “leadership”.

The short version: I like the place where biology, hardware, and systems thinking meet, and I am trying to be steady, useful, and honest. This site is my record of whether I actually do that.
