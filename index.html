About Noble Slate

Now

Hello there. My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my corner of the internet. It is part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I did something, it will be shown here. If I am still learning, I’ll say that.

This site is called Noble Slate, pulled out of my own name “George-Owen”. “George” comes from an old Greek root meaning something like “earth-worker” or farmer (the same root you see explained on sites like Behind the Name). “Owen” is tied to names that mean “well-born” or “noble” (see the Owen entry). A slate is a fine-grained rock that holds a straight line under pressure and used to be a literal writing surface in classrooms (more on slate). Put it together and you get how I try to work on things: pressure-tested, but always re-writable. That’s the point of this page and the name.

Right now

I am drafting a simple thrust-vector-controlled model rocket I can recover completely after use. I am working on two custom servo-gimbal sketches, a PID controller outline, an IMU short list, a first control-loop plan, and a ground test checklist. Failure notes will live openly on this site instead of being hidden.

I am also developing an early wearable health device called VitaTrace. I’ve drafted the basic concept, rough service flow, and what the product should actually do in practice. My father’s passing from pancreatic cancer when I was younger is what pushed me toward this direction, so I care a lot about catching quiet early signals and not letting subtle changes turn into a life-or-death decision that could have been avoided.

On the music side, I am getting more confident on tuba. The tuba is the big low brass anchor at the bottom of the band mix — I like how it quietly holds everything up. There is a log on how to play tuba on this site, written so a beginner can follow it.
