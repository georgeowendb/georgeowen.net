Now

My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my corner of the internet. It’s part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I did something, it goes here. If I’m still learning, I’ll say that.

This site is called Noble Slate for a reason:

  1. George traces back to an old Greek root for “earth worker”.
  2. Owen is linked to “well-born” or “noble”.
  3. A slate is a rock that holds a straight line under pressure, and a surface you can write on, erase, and write on again.

Put that together and you get how I want to work: steady, useful, and willing to rewrite things in public. That’s the point of this page.

Right now

Rocket: I’m drafting a simple TVC model rocket I can recover cleanly. Two-servo gimbal sketches, a rough PID controller, an IMU short list, a first control loop draft, and a basic ground test plan are in progress. When things fail (they will), the failure notes will live here.

VitaTrace: I’m sketching an early wearable health concept called VitaTrace. I’m writing a clear problem statement, a basic service flow, and some notes on privacy for Ontario. My dad died of pancreatic cancer when I was very young. That’s why I care about early signals and not just “sick vs not sick”.

Music: I’m getting more solid on tuba. I log breath work, long tones, and practice time, and rewrite notes so a beginner could follow them without wanting to quit.

Footnotes

  1. George → from Greek geōrgos (“earth worker”, farmer).
  2. Owen → often linked to Welsh Owain / Latin Eugenius (“well-born”, “noble”).
  3. Slate → a fine-grained metamorphic rock with slaty cleavage: it splits into thin, flat sheets that hold a straight edge. It has been used for roofing and classroom writing slates. The combination of pressure and rewriting is the part I care about.
