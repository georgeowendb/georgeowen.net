About Noble Slate

Now

Hello there. My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my corner of the internet. It is part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I did something, it should show up here somewhere. If I am still learning something, it will be mentioned here as well.[1]

This site is called Noble Slate. The name comes out of my own name, “George-Owen”. “George” traces back to a Greek root for an earth worker or farmer. “Owen” is linked to names that are usually glossed as “well-born” or “noble”. Slate is a rock that holds a straight edge under pressure, or a surface you write on, erase, and write on again. When you put that together, you get how I get my work done, why is by putting in the effort, holding shape under pressure, and still be willing to rewrite when the data changes.[2]

Right now

I am drafting a simple thrust-vector-controlled model rocket that I can recover completely after flight. I am working on two custom servo gimbal sketches, a PID controller outline, an IMU short list, a first control-loop plan, the overall design and frame of the rocket and later, a ground test checklist. Failure notes will be posted here, not hidden in a notebook.[3]

I am also developing an early wearable health device called VitaTrace. I have a developped a clear concept, a basic service flow, and an outline of what the device should measure and how. My father’s death from pancreatic cancer when I was younger is what pushed me in this direction, which is why I care and am interested in EWS.[4]

I am getting more confident on tuba as well. I keep a practice log for breath work, long tones, and time on the horn. There is a beginner-friendly tuba page on this site, written for someone who has never touched a brass instrument before.[5]

Last updated: November 07, 2025.

I write pages in Markdown and publish plain HTML. The CSS is deliberately minimal, so the site loads quickly and stays readable on weak connections and older hardware. At the moment it is just static files on GitHub Pages in front of Cloudflare.[6]
