About this site

My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. I am in Grade 10 in the Extended French program at Holy Trinity Catholic High School in Simcoe, Ontario. This site is the place where I keep track of what I am building and what I am learning, in public, so I can see the whole trail later.[2]

The name Noble Slate comes from my own name. George comes from a root for an earth worker or farmer, someone who turns ground over by hand. Owen is usually glossed as well born or noble. Slate is a rock that keeps a clean edge under pressure and is also something you can write on, erase, then write on again. The name reminds me to do the work, hold shape when things are loud, and still be willing to rewrite if the facts say I was wrong.[3]

Right now

Most of my time goes into a few long term projects that sit at the intersection of hardware, medicine, and flight:

  • Azimuth v1 is a thrust vector controlled model rocket with onboard logging. I am experimenting with a simple gimbal, an IMU, and a basic PID loop so I can see how the rocket actually rotates in flight instead of guessing from video.
  • VitaTrace is a wearable biosensor idea that started after my father died from pancreatic cancer. I am logging simple signals like heart rate and motion and trying to understand what an honest early warning system would need to look like, even at a student level.
  • Nullvector is a desk sized attitude control stand. It lets me tune control code on the bench first and only put it on a rocket once I know how it behaves.
  • Blackwatch is a small stress logger, a kind of black box for humans. It records heart rate, movement, and environment during things like drills or workouts, then I look at how my body actually reacts under load.[4]

At school I am on student council, help run the morning announcements and A V setup, play euphonium and tuba in concert band, curl, and build with the VEX robotics team. I also write for the school newspaper and go to a health care club that brings in real practitioners to talk about their work.[5]

Outside school I am a cadet with 104 Starfighter Royal Canadian Air Cadet Squadron. It is where I am learning how to work inside a chain of command, handle uniform and drill, and look after younger cadets once I have more experience.

Direction

Long term I want to work somewhere between aerospace medicine, mechatronics, and human health in extreme environments. That might look like research on how to keep crews healthy in orbit, in aircraft, or in remote locations on Earth.

These projects are small and early, but they keep me moving toward that direction. Every build adds one more thing I understand with my hands, not just on paper.

How this site works

Pages start as notes in plain text. I write in Markdown, render to HTML, and style with a small amount of CSS. There is no framework, no build pipeline, and no client side extras outside of the search wrapper.

The site lives on static hosting so it stays fast even on weak connections and older hardware. If the stack ever changes, the rule is that it must stay simple enough that I can fix it alone on a weekend.[6]

When I add detailed project logs, they will show up on the Projects page. When I write longer pieces or notes, they will land on the Writing page. The Now page is for what I am doing this month, not what I did years ago.
