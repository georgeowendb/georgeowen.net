Now

Hello there. My name is George-Owen Damoyi-Bouridis. This page is my logbook and my corner of this internet. It is part portfolio, part notebook, and part memory for future me. If I did something, it goes here. If I am still learning, I will say that.

This site is called Noble Slate for a simple reason:

  • George traces back to an old Greek root for “earth worker”.
  • Owen links to “well-born,” often read as “noble”.
  • A slate is a rock that holds a straight line under pressure, and a surface you can write on, erase, and write on again.

Put it together and you get how I want to work: do the ground-level work, stay steady under pressure, and be willing to rewrite my thinking in public. That is the point of this page.

Right now

Rocket: I am drafting a simple TVC model rocket I can recover cleanly. Two-servo gimbal sketches, a rough PID controller, an IMU short list, a first control loop plan, and a short ground test plan are in progress. Failure notes will be linked on this site instead of being hidden.

VitaTrace: I am currently developing an early wearable health concept called VitaTrace. I am preparing a clear problem statement, a basic service flow, and privacy notes for Ontario. My father passed away from pancreatic cancer when I was very young. That is why I care about early signals, not just a vague label of “sick” or “not sick”.

Music: I am currently getting more proficient at tuba. I log breath work, long tones, and practice time, and I rewrite notes so a beginner could follow them without wanting to quit.

Footnotes
  1. George → Greek geōrgos (“earth worker” / farmer).
  2. Owen → commonly linked to Welsh Owain / Latin Eugenius (“well-born,” “noble”).
  3. Slate → a fine-grained metamorphic rock formed under low-grade heat and pressure. It develops slaty cleavage, so it splits into thin, flat sheets that keep a straight edge. It was used for roofing and for classroom writing slates.
